Before we begin

A few things
worth knowing

This experience won't tell you what to feel or when to feel it. It will just ask you to keep going until something becomes clear. Trust that.

Your beliefs were not mistakes. They were your mind's best attempt to keep you safe with the information it had. Most of us are more resilient and adaptive than we know — complex beings with shadow and light.

About the roots

Your earliest beliefs weren't reasoned out. They were written into your nervous system before you had the capacity to question them. In early childhood, what you experienced simply was true — not because you were naive, but because that is how perception works before the mind develops the distance to doubt. Words came later. The belief came first.

The roots were there long before you could see the fruits. The trunk — the experiences of your life — did not create the roots. It revealed and reinforced what was already planted. This work does not plant anything new. It helps you see what has been growing the whole time.

This work is not about deciding whether you are broken or whole. It is about choosing, with fuller awareness, what you do next.

Root Note

Part of this work is learning what is truly yours and what belongs to someone else. You did not choose the experiences that shaped your early beliefs. You do get to choose what you do with them now.

Information is not transformation. This experience is the bridge.

Part One

The Fruits

Fruits are what's visible — the patterns, behaviors, and reactions that keep showing up no matter how hard you try to change them. We start here not because the fruits are the problem, but because they are the door.

Answer all four prompts. Then distill your fruits. You'll choose one to follow all the way to its root.

What patterns keep showing up in your life, no matter how hard you try to change them?

Root Note

'I always end up doing the work alone.' 'I say yes when I mean no.' 'I shut down when things get close.' Name them plainly.

What do the people closest to you reflect back to you that you resist hearing?

Where do you feel most stuck right now?

When you start to feel the emotion you named, what do you notice yourself doing? This might be something you reach for, something you avoid, or a way you behave that seems to happen almost automatically — before you've even decided to do it.

Root Note

These patterns are not character flaws. They are responses that once made sense. You are not being asked to judge them — only to name them.

Part Two

The Trunk

You are working with this fruit:

The trunk holds the experiences that revealed and reinforced the root — the moments that confirmed what your nervous system already suspected was true about you. We use emotion and the body to find our way there.

Body check-in — start here

Bring the fruit you chose to mind. Sit with it for a moment before writing.

Where do you feel it in your body right now? Chest, stomach, throat, jaw, shoulders? You don't have to like the feeling — just notice it.

Staying with that sensation — what emotion or feeling word goes with it, even if it surprises you?

Root Note

Anger almost always has something underneath it — usually fear or grief. If anger came up, ask: what am I afraid of losing, or what have I already lost?

What memories come up when you sit with this feeling? Any association that surfaces is the right one — your nervous system is pointing you somewhere.

Root Note

You don't have to understand why a memory is coming up. Just write it down. The connection will become clearer as you go.

Who was there in those early memories? What was said — or not said — that landed in you?

What did those experiences confirm about you — even silently, even without words?

Root Note

Stay with yourself here. Not what it said about them — about you. Even if it feels unfair, what did some part of you quietly conclude?

When you're ready, distill your trunk experiences into labels for your tree.

Part Three

The Roots

The trunk experiences you found:

Roots are the core beliefs that have been operating underground — formed before you had words, written into your nervous system before you could question them. They are not the truth. But they have been acting like it.

Look at the trunk experiences above. What did those moments tell you about you — not about them, not about the world — specifically about you?

Root Note

Watch for the jump. 'She read my journal' tells you what happened. 'My thoughts are disgusting, so I must be disgusting' tells you what you believed it meant about you. Keep going until it's about you.

Now ask again — and what does that mean about you?

Root Note

Each answer will get shorter and more essential. You are looking for the floor — the place where there's nothing underneath anymore. Keep asking.

Body check-in

Check in with your body again. Has something shifted? Sometimes when we get close to a root there is a feeling of recognition — like a puzzle piece clicking into place. The image gets a little bigger, a little clearer.

What are you noticing right now?

Write the root as simply as you can. As few words as possible.

Root Note

Roots sound like: 'I am not enough.' 'I am a burden.' 'I cannot be trusted.' One sentence. Or less.

When you're ready, distill your root belief.

Part Four

The Truth

The root you found:

The old root is still there. It is strong. Naming it does not remove it. But you can plant something alongside it. A tree growing crooked on a hill does not straighten overnight when replanted on level ground — but it does, slowly, begin to grow toward the light.

What information was the root belief missing? What didn't the younger version of you know yet?

What would you say to a child who believed this about themselves?

What is the most honest and most generous thing that could be true about you today, instead of the belief you started with?

Root Note

This is not positive thinking. It is not replacing a lie with a prettier lie. It is asking: given everything you now know, what is more true than what you believed?

Write the new truth as simply as you can.

When you're ready, distill your new truth.
